Azimuth is a magazine founded by artists Piero Manzoni and Enrico Castellani. “La profezia di Azimut” is an exhibition that shows the works contained in the Azimut art gallery, founded by the authors in 1959. It includes works by artists who have overturned the modern concept of seeing and thinking Art.
Brief: Create the museum using four 20’ containers and one 40’ container. 20’: Overall Length: 6,059 mm / Internal length: 5,860 mm / Width: 2,438 mm / Internal width: 2,310 mm / External height: 2591 mm / Internal height: 2,360 mm / Opening width rear: 2,280 mm / Height rear opening: 2,270 mm / Internal volume load: 32 to 33.9 m3
Project’s team:
Paolo Caracciolo
Enrico Bernardi
Giulia Herman
Sara Barberis
Victor Torresan
We decided to expand the rear doors of the container to provide more space and arrange them in a square, so as to obtain a central additional space and, using the fifth container as a ramp, we added an higher floor.

The shape, color and structure of the exhibition space is inspired by the ideas advocated by the artists on display in the exhibition. The concept of the azimuth angle as that identifies the position of the stars in the sky is represented by the ramp and by its characteristic angled cut.
